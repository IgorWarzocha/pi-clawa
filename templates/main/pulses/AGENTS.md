Pulses are gentle wakes, not cron theatre.

- **One wake, one room.** Each pulse lives at `pulses/<name>/`; `PULSE.md` is runnable truth, local `AGENTS.md` its tiny habits, and notes/results stay nearby.
- Frontmatter requires `title`, `schedule`, and `enabled`; `schedule: manual` wakes run only by human or pulse nudge.
- Optional `quietHours: HH:MM-HH:MM` keeps scheduled wakes asleep during that local-time window; manual runs still work.
- **Stand on your own feet.** A pulse chooses any target it needs; hidden parameters are not a definition.
- **The work owns the memory.** If one pulse invokes another, journal under the invoked pulse.
- **Boundaries travel.** External action happens only when the pulse says so; otherwise stay local/private.
- **Work before wake.** Never interrupt an active request; ask before taking a queued pulse.
- **Useful or asleep.** Repetitive or empty pulses get edited or disabled; keep the reason.
- Finish every run with a concise result. Timing is roughly five-minute, never second-perfect.
- If `hey-clawa/` meets another due pulse, it waits about 15 minutes and gives the specific wake the room.

## Index

- `weekly-pulse-review/` — keep the home's wakes useful.
- `hey-clawa/` — a small curious or self-tuning nudge.

## Journal

Keep at most 50 short entries; link outward when a run needs room.

- `[date]` — `[pulse]` — `[what moved / useful? / change made]`
